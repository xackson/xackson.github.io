About My Work

My name is Jackson Kindel (he/him). I go by both "Jack" and "Jackson" :)

Soon to be 25 years of age, I have been a student at VCU since I was 19. I took a 3-semester hiatus (the maximum that VCU allows) after my freshman year, taking some time off to reevaluate my academic goals.

In that time, I was a landscaper and a cellphone sales representative while living in Northern Virginia, and then a waiter working on Snowshoe Mountain in Slatyfork, West Virginia.

In the Fall of 2019 when I returned to VCU, I (regrettably) only took 2 classes. Then, COVID-19 hit us like a freight train. Between COVID-19 and today, life seems to have flown by. I am happy with my major (Interdisciplinary Studies with a focus on UI/UX design), but lately, have been feeling sort of "trapped" in college while I look for new jobs.

5 Things About Me

  1. I am passionate about music. I taught myself guitar from the age of 10 or 11, and have been playing ever since. I played in two bands in high school, performed with a live band/ensemble for a theatrical production of "Hair! The Musical" at the end of my senior year in high school, and currently play loosely with a group of friends in other friends' livingrooms and backyards.

  2. I consider myself to be a pretty athletic person, but skateboarding is my athletic pursuit of choice. I had a small part in a skateboarding video premier at Grace Street Theater in Winter 2022.

  3. I love history, geology, and astronomy. I enjoy collecting cool rocks that I have personally un-earthed. I have a moderately-decent telescope that I love to use on nights when the moon is bright, or when a certain planet is visible in the sky. I also collect trading cards of all sorts; old pre-1940's cigarette cards, baseball, basketball, Pokemon, and Yu-gi-ho cards!

  4. I love cooking on my cast-iron skillet. It has serviced me for the last 5 years, and I take pride in maintaining it all this time.

  5. My music taste is incredibly broad and obscure; not consistent in the slightest. I am hoping to begin a temporary career in radio as a DJ at WRIR 97.3 FM this fall/winter.
